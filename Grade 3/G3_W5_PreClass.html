Where Shall We Meet? Finalising the Trip

Prepositions of time & place · Wh- questions (review) · making suggestions

📍 This week's story: It's finally the morning of Tom and Maya's weekend trip! Before they set off, they need to agree on the exact time and place to meet, check the forecast, and make a few last-minute suggestions. Read their final messages carefully — this is the last piece of the puzzle before they go.

3Grammar Focus

3.1 — Prepositions of Time: At / On / In

Three "sizes" of time
Think of it as small (a moment) → medium (a day) → large (a period) — AT is the smallest, IN is the biggest.
PrepositionUsed forExamples
atexact clock times, and a few fixed expressionsat 9am, at noon, at night, at the weekend
ondays and dateson Saturday, on 5th July, on my birthday
inparts of the day, months, seasons, yearsin the morning, in July, in summer, in 2024

3.2 — Prepositions of Place (Extended)

You already know "in", "on" and "at" for place from earlier grades. This week we add more precise prepositions for describing exactly where something is.

The station scene: prepositions of place in action
Tom is standing "next to" the ticket machine, which is "in front of" the entrance, which is "between" the two shops.
PrepositionMeaningExample
next to / besidedirectly at the side ofI'll be next to the ticket machine.
in front offacing the front of somethingIt's in front of the main entrance.
behindat the back of somethingThe car park is behind the station.
betweenwith one thing on each sideIt's between the coffee stand and the shop.
oppositefacing something, across from itThe hotel is opposite the beach.
neara short distance fromWe're staying near the station.

3.3 — Wh- Questions: A Quick Review

You already know these question words from earlier grades. Here they are again, in a travel context.

Question wordAsks aboutExample
WhereplaceWhere shall we meet?
When / What timetimeWhat time does the train leave?
WhopersonWho else is coming?
WhyreasonWhy don't we grab a coffee first?
Howmanner/methodHow are we getting there?
How much / How manyquantityHow much does the ticket cost?

⚠️ Common Errors at This Level

❌ Incorrect✅ Correct
Let's meet in Monday.Let's meet on Monday.
The train leaves on 9am.The train leaves at 9am.
I was born at July.I was born in July.

Rule of thumb: think about the "size" of the time expression. A precise clock time or a fixed phrase like "at night" → at. A day or date → on. Anything longer (a part of the day, a month, a season, a year) → in.

4Making Suggestions

Tom and Maya use three common structures for suggesting an idea. Notice that two of them use a gerund after a preposition — exactly the pattern from Week 3!

StructureExampleNote
Let's + base verbLet's meet at 8:45.no "to", no "-ing"
Why don't we + base verbWhy don't we grab a coffee first?a question form, but really a suggestion
How about / What about + gerundHow about meeting 15 minutes early?preposition "about" + "-ing" — recycled from Week 3!

All three structures are common and natural — native speakers switch between them freely in conversation. Using a mix, instead of only "Let's...", will make your English sound much more natural.

7Error Clinic

Read each sentence. Some have mistakes — fix them. One sentence is already correct — do not "fix" it if it isn't broken.

  1. "Let's meet in Monday."
    Incorrect — days take "on": "Let's meet on Monday."
  2. "The train leaves on 9am."
    Incorrect — exact clock times take "at": "The train leaves at 9am."
  3. "The ticket machine is in front of the entrance."
    This sentence is already correct — no error here. "In front of" is used correctly here. Resist the urge to change anything.
  4. "How about go for a coffee first?"
    Incorrect — "about" is a preposition, so it needs a gerund (recycled from Week 3!): "How about going for a coffee first?"
  5. "I was born at 1998."
    Incorrect — years take "in": "I was born in 1998."

🗣Speaking Practice

Meeting someone means agreeing on a time and a place — at six, on Monday, at the station — and making suggestions until everyone says yes. That's this week's speaking, out loud. Voice memo app ready!

Roleplay scenario

Let's meet!

Role A: You want to meet your friend this week. Suggest a day, a time, and a place: "Shall we meet on Thursday? Let's say at half past six, at the main station — at the meeting point near the ticket machines?"

Role B: The first idea doesn't work for you. Say why, and suggest a change: "I can't on Thursday — how about Friday? And let's meet at the café instead, it's warmer." Agree in the end and repeat the final plan.

How: With a partner, or both roles yourself; record 2 minutes. When you listen back, check the little words: AT six, ON Friday, IN the evening, AT the station. They're small, but everyone hears them.
Sentence challenge

In / on / at — six true facts

Say six TRUE sentences about your own week — each one with a time or place preposition. Up to 60 seconds each:

  • Something you do in the morning
  • Something you do on Sundays
  • Something that starts at an exact time
  • A place you often are at
  • Something in your city that is on a famous street
  • Where you were born (in + city/country, in + year)
How: One recording, timer on. When you listen back, check the rule of size: in for big (months, years, cities), on for days and streets, at for exact times and points.
Shadowing drill

Copy the dialogue

Choose 4 lines from this week's dialogue — two suggestions and two answers. Read them aloud three times:

  • Slowly and clearly;
  • at normal speed;
  • with real feeling — like you really want to meet this friend!
How: Record the third time. When you listen back, check: "Shall we…? ↗" goes up; "Let's…" goes down. Suggestions have a melody.

Tips: never miss a meeting

  • Always repeat the final plan — day, time, place: "OK: Friday, six thirty, at the café." Three words can save a whole evening.
  • Learn the suggestion trio by heart: Shall we…? / Let's… / How about…? Three keys, every door.
  • Weather talk + a suggestion is the most natural invitation in English: "It's going to be sunny on Saturday — shall we go to the park?"

9Explore More: Build Your Own Independence

Use these tasks to notice this week's language in real English before your next class.

  • Real forecast check: Search online for tomorrow's weather forecast in a city of your choice. Write 3 sentences using "in the morning/afternoon/evening" and the actual temperatures in degrees.
  • More prepositions of time: Look up "during", "since", "for", and "until" — these are closely related to at/on/in but do a different job. Write one example sentence for each.
  • Suggestion collection: Watch a short video or read a conversation in English where people plan to meet. Note down every suggestion structure used (Let's, why don't we, how about, shall we, what if we...).
  • Describe your area: Write 3 sentences describing where things are near your home, using next to / opposite / between / behind / in front of.
  • Speaking challenge: Arrange a real (or imaginary) meeting with a friend or classmate out loud, using at least 2 suggestion structures and both types of prepositions from this week.
