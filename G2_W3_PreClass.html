3Grammar Focus

3.1 — There Is / There Are

We use there is (one thing) and there are (more than one thing) to say what exists somewhere.

FormExample
There is / there's + singular nounThere's a large kitchen.
There are + plural nounThere are two bedrooms.
negative: there isn't / there aren'tThere isn't any parking.
question: Is there...? / Are there...?Is there a bus stop nearby?
Wh- question: How many...are there?How many rooms are there?

⚠️ Match "Is/Are" to the Noun!

❌ Incorrect✅ Correct
There is two bedrooms.There are two bedrooms.
There are a kitchen.There is a kitchen.
How many rooms there are?How many rooms are there?

In a Wh- question, "are there" (not "there are") — the word order flips, just like in other questions.

🔎 Check Your Understanding

Do we use "there is" or "there are" for "two bedrooms"? There are.
Do we use "there is" or "there are" for "a kitchen"? There is.
In the question "How many rooms ___ there?", do we say "are" or "there are"? Are — "How many rooms are there?"

These question words are useful for house hunting.

3.3 — Prepositions of Place (Preview)

These small words tell us exactly where something is. This week, we look at four of the most common ones.

Pablo's living room: where is everything?
There's a picture on the wall. There's a plant behind the sofa (you can only see the top of it). There's a lamp next to the sofa, and a table in front of it, on the rug.
PrepositionExample
inThe sofa is in the living room.
onThere's a picture on the wall.
next toThe lamp is next to the sofa.
behindThe plant is behind the sofa.

🔎 Check Your Understanding

Is the lamp far from the sofa, or right beside it? Right beside it — "next to" means very close.
If the plant is "behind" the sofa, can you see it easily from the front? No — it's hidden behind.

6Error Clinic

Read each sentence. Some have mistakes — fix them. One sentence is already correct — do not "fix" it if it isn't broken.

  1. "There is two bedrooms."
    Incorrect — plural needs "are": "There are two bedrooms."
  2. "There are a kitchen."
    Incorrect — singular needs "is": "There is a kitchen." (or "There's a kitchen.")
  3. "There isn't any parking."
    This sentence is already correct — no error here. "There isn't" correctly matches the negative, uncountable use with "any". Resist the urge to change anything.
  4. "How many rooms there are?"
    Incorrect word order — questions need "are there": "How many rooms are there?"
  5. "Is there a bus stop nearby?"
    Also correct. "Is there" is correctly placed at the start of the question. A second trap — not every sentence needs a correction.

🗣Speaking Practice

Pablo is looking at flats and asking: Is there a balcony? Are there two bedrooms? This week you describe rooms and homes out loud — yours and dream ones. Voice memo app ready!

Describe your room

One minute: where I live

Stand in one room of your home (really!). Set a timer for 1 minute and describe what you see: "There's a sofa next to the window. There are two lamps. There isn't a TV…"

How: Record while looking around — real things make real sentences. When you listen back, check the pair: there IS a lamp (one), there ARE two chairs (more).
Roleplay scenario

Viewing a flat

Role A — Looking for a flat (like Pablo): Ask five questions: "Is there a balcony? Are there any shops near here? Is there a washing machine?"

Role B — Owner: Answer honestly about YOUR real home: "Yes, there is. / No, there isn't, but there's a…" Then change roles.

How: With a partner, or both roles yourself; record 2 minutes. When you listen back, check the short answers: "Yes, there is" — not just "yes."
Sentence challenge

Six furniture words, one sentence each

Say one sentence with each word — where it is in YOUR home (or why you don't have one!). Up to 60 seconds each:

  • a sofa
  • a wardrobe
  • a mirror
  • a fridge
  • a shelf
  • a lamp
How: One recording, timer on. When you listen back, check the place words: next to, under, on, in the corner.

Tips: describing places

  • There's is how real people say there is: "There's a café downstairs." One smooth sound.
  • Describe rooms in a circle: start at the door, go around. Your listener never gets lost.
  • Play the memory game: look at any photo of a room for ten seconds, close it, and say what there is. Great practice, anywhere.
